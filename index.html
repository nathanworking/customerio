On my life, what I do, and why Customer.io 

why

When I came across the post for Customer.io I was excited. No, like really excited! I went to fill out the questions and I thought: I should give you more information about me, in addition to the questions of course. More importantly, I should prove to you what I can do and why I’m excited. Bonus: I’ll share my special connection to Oregon :)

Why

Customer.io?

f

1

The work sounds amazing

The best thing about reading through the description of this job wasn’t that the work sounds amazing, it’s that I know it is. I’ve done every single thing on your list when I worked at a company called Total Package Hockey. I honestly never thought I'd find another job like that, so it's pretty exciting to me.

2

Did I mention excitement yet?

I didn’t even apply to the job. I mean, I will when you’re reading this but while I’m writing it I haven’t applied. Despite that, it’s so exciting! The idea of this work with a small team focused on building really quality experiences to communicate with people is something I’m simply excited about.

3

Teams are always better

I’ve worked in interesting situations. Monthly contracting, straight freelancing, full-time teams and most everything in between. At one point I worked full time for a hockey company while working for a marketing agency. Fun times. Teams are always so much more fun to me. Especially small teams. Is that a reason? Basically just “it sounds fun?” Feels like a pretty good one to me.

4

Learning is awesome

Learning has always been my favorite part of work. Mostly because it truly never feels like work. Am I really getting paid to learn this? I love saying that. The best example of my ability to learn is my Unpslash profile. I became super interested in photography barely a year ago. Now I have better photos than I started with and a cool web project I love getting to do every week.

Fun note!

e

I love the Amiverse!

These little characters are awesome! Okay, you all knew that already. I actually bring this up to point out a weakness of mine: Illustration. I don’t just want to make Ami though. I love landing pages that incorporate awesome illustrations and I want to be able to set up my pages with fun things (Amis included).

This is definitely the first new skill I’d learn as part of the Customer.io team. I’m actually already working on it :)
